Postdoctoral fellow
Statistics Department
Harvard University

I am interested in adaptive intervention in the mobile health space. I build computational tools that analyze human behavior from phone data (e.g., sensors, self-input) and then intervenes in real time in ways that are personalized and contextualized to users' needs, daily routine, and the environments they live in.

Keywords: Adaptive health intervention, Mobile Sensing, Context-aware computing, Reinforcement leanring, User-centered design

Short bio
I am a postdoc at Harvard University, where I am advised by Susan Murphy. I completed my PhD at Cornell University. My PhD advisor was Tanzeem Chourdhury. I also had the opportunity to work with many amazing collaborators (Andrew Campbell, Predrag Klasnja, Nic Lane, Mi Zhang, Lama Nachman). Project highlights

MyBehavior

MyBehavior understands a user's dietary intake and physical activity routine, and suggests small changes that personalize to the user's lifestyle. MyBehavior is one of the first apps that combines sensor data with reinforcement learning to provide personalized health recommendations.

See our JMIR and Ubicomp 2015 papers for more details about MyBehavior.



See how MyBehavior app works

SARA

SARA (Substance Abuse Research Assistant) provides intervention to increase self-reported data collection in mobile health. SARA tackles the problem of low engagement rates in existing mobile health apps. SARA uses a micro-randomized trial to evaluate a variety of engagement strategies.

See our ubicomp workshop paper for more details about SARA.
